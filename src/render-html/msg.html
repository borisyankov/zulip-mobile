Brock Whittaker
8:32 AM

Is there really a concern with brute forcing passwords in local networks? The key to a local network is that users within are generally trusted and part of an organization, and logs are discoverable by admins easily to find malicious activity (and if admins wanted to log into a user's account they could just change the password hash to a known one in postgres anyways).

I think rate limiting is probably good but more from the perspective of people not being able to ddos the cloud hosted version, and less from a password brute forcing risk.
